As temperature increases, particles move faster and push with more force. To keep the pressure from increasing, the plunger moves automatically (expanding the volume) making room for the particles.

Did you know...? Mathematically, the volume reaches 0 at Absolute Zero, but in real life this is impossible! Matter occupies physical space. In reality, before reaching that temperature, the gas would condense into a liquid and stop obeying Charles' Law. This is the limit of the "Ideal Gas" model.

Statement of the Law:

"At constant pressure, the volume occupied by a fixed mass of gas is directly proportional to its absolute temperature (in Kelvin)."
